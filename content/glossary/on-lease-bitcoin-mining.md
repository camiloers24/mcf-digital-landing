On-lease Bitcoin mining refers to the deployment of Bitcoin mining equipment directly at an oil and gas production site — on the same lease as the wellhead — using power generated from the site's own gas supply rather than drawing from the electrical grid.

## How it differs from conventional mining

In a conventional mining operation, ASICs (application-specific integrated circuit miners) are housed in data centers or hosted facilities connected to grid power. In an on-lease deployment, the power source is co-located with the compute: gas is combusted in a generator at the pad, the generator produces electricity, and that electricity runs the mining hardware in a containerized unit placed beside it.

This co-location eliminates two major cost layers: transmission infrastructure (pipelines) and electricity transmission (grid interconnect). The result is a closed-loop system in which stranded or flared gas that previously had no commercial outlet becomes the fuel source for Bitcoin production.

## Key distinctions

The term is distinct from:

- **Hosted mining** — where a miner pays a third party for power and facilities at an off-site location
- **Grid-connected mining** — where miners use purchased grid electricity at a fixed industrial rate

On-lease mining is defined by the physical co-location of the gas source, the generator, and the mining equipment on a single production site.
